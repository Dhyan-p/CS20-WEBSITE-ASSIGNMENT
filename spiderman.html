Spider-Man

Spider-Man, also known as Peter Parker, is one of the most beloved superheroes in the world. Created in 1962, he quickly became popular because he was a relatable teenager dealing with everyday problems. After being bitten by a radioactive spider, Peter gained powers such as wall-climbing, super agility, spider-sense, and enhanced strength. His homemade costume and web-shooters show his intelligence and creativity.

Spider-Man's story emphasizes responsibility. After Peter lost his Uncle Ben, he learned the famous lesson: “With great power comes great responsibility.” This moment shaped his mission to fight crime and help people, even when it made his life harder. Spider-Man protects New York City from villains like the Green Goblin, Doctor Octopus, and Venom.

A big part of Spider-Man's appeal is his struggle to balance life as a hero and as a regular person. He juggles school, work, friendships, and battles with dangerous enemies. His challenges make him feel real and human, and many fans see themselves in Peter's struggles.

Throughout comics and movies, Spider-Man's bravery and kindness have made him a symbol of hope. He always tries to do the right thing, even when the odds are against him. His humor, determination, and heart make him one of Marvel's most inspiring heroes.
